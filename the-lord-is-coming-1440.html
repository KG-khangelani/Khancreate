The Lord is Coming

Everyday I pray for mankind
We’re all slaves to a generation socialized, and sickness is in the mind
We are habitual thinkers, substance abusers and habitual drinkers
But free your mind because the plug is watching from the top floor
Hoping you too would fall for the illusions of a temporary high

What we think we need to get by
But are we really trying when kids are dying and depression is trending?
Are these the signs of an Armageddon?
We’re all in denial and it’s all cool until you’re suicidal
We never learn from our so-called idols, we’re just repeating the cycle, so
I never read the news anymore, it’s hard not to feel hopeless
When no one noticed, the explosion came from a man whose legal gun was loaded
It’s all watered down and sugarcoated
We voted but did we really get to choose?
We don’t need proof that they separate us from the truth
Mothers and fathers are being separated from their youth
And they’re selling the American Dream and what it appears to be
But they don’t teach us that wealth is unattainable with their currency
History, is not my brother’s story
The original founders were buried in the ground where men have planted seeds of disease
And they’ve justified being thieves
Feeding their inner demons and blaming the minorities
It’s a World War III, corruption versus greed
Not you versus me
But do we ever think, of the need for inner peace?
They can’t put a price on your soul, don’t matter your religion
Right and wrong is something everybody knows
They pick and choose what’s equal
Who’s good and who’s evil
And this is the devil’s world but the Lord is coming for his people

Poem by Gabriella Thopson (H.E.R)
Another year of pandemic trounced, distraught and disillusioned youths bare forward as they bravely, undeterred, still fight forwards. In the wake of perils, strife, and clouded future outcomes, these brave creative soldiers once again tear through the shackles of hindrances strewn on their future paths of success. Landmines of loss and hardships stir their concerns, still, they bear forward, as they unrelentingly fight through their poetic apocalypse. The Multimedia Design Studies 3rd year at the Academy is hard at work in completing this year’s annual short film, collectively titled Poetic Apocalypse. This year’s short film takes on a different spin - it will combine film, animation, and motion graphics, encapsulated by narrated poems of famous writers, the 2021 film short-film promises to be exciting, interactive, and very different than before.
Introduction
To build a momentum with colour. The symbolism of the colour palette is fire flames, as the end times are described biblically as a burning furnace. Suggested by the tittle that the poet’s context is religious based. Ranging from darker tones to brighter with the inclusion of green as life is a major aspect of the storyline. From green to flame then black and grey ashes. The colour palette is extracted from the ‘Lightbox’ photography series by a talented photographer Elizabeth Horsely.
~ Elizabeth
“The aim of this colour series was to explore the relationship of different colours and colour combinations to evoke emotive responses, through the process of painting with Lightbox sculptures…”
The goal is to explore the meaning of ~ Apocalypse ~ through the
execution of a moving film.

To imagine an apocalypse. Gabriella’s poem was a better description of
what comes to mind when thinking about an Apocalypse. That the end time are now, we need to wake up and see the signs. Often our imagination of the end is populated with western biblical scenes while neglecting that the end is in process now and is yet to result from the acts of now, from the way we live life today.
Goals & Objctives
To pave the way and roughly visualise the poem as storyboard board. This is done also to experiment on visual composition to figure the best way to communicate the intension.
