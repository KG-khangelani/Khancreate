Privacy includes some control
over some information about
us and who can experience us.
88% respondents never use Facebook privacy settings to protect their data.
This may be because users do not know that these settings exist or may not know how to activate them, which may leave their personal data vulnerable to any
potential profiling.
67% of Facebook users' personal data are partially available, while 33%
have their full personal details available.

Facebook does not put a default block on new users' personal information
when they sign up to be a member on the site, which makes it easier for
users to view each other's information, and also makes it possible for those
with malicious intent to obtain sensitive data.
The campaign aims to provoke Facebook users into making a fuss over the platform’s privacy policy that perceives the user into thinking that their privacy is not serious matter.

The campaign's objective is to help people aged 35-40 understand the significance of personal data(privacy). To produce the needed motion
graphics for users to start making a fuss over Facebook's privacy policy that exploits their privacy.
People are starting to make a fuss on
Facebook and their lack of respect for
privacy.
Sir, it is necessary to bring a little change
in Facebook account privacy, because
anyone an easily hack our account.
I am done with Facebook! I can not
continue to support a company like
@Facebook
